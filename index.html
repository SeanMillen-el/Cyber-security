Cyber-security

A cybersecurity class teaches students how to protect computer systems, networks, and data from cyber threats. It covers topics like ethical hacking, encryption, malware defense, and digital forensics through hands-on labs and theoretical lessons. Students also learn about cybersecurity laws, best practices, and real-world security challenges to prepare for careers in the field.

What will you learn?

  • Fundamentals of cybercrime and digital forensics
  • Basic coding and software development
  • Introduction to cryptography systems and their history
  • Networking fundamentals and internet structure

Coursework

Hands-On Ethical Hacking & Penetration Testing

Learn how to think like a hacker by conducting penetration tests, exploiting vulnerabilities in controlled environments, and using tools like Metasploit, Nmap, and Wireshark to assess system security.

Defensive Security & Threat Mitigation

Develop strategies to defend against cyber threats by configuring firewalls, implementing intrusion detection systems, analyzing malware, and responding to simulated cyberattacks to strengthen overall security posture.

Digital Forensics & Incident Response

Investigate cyber incidents by analyzing digital evidence, tracing attack origins, and using forensic tools to recover compromised data, all while understanding legal and ethical considerations in cybersecurity investigations.

Cybersecurity is a fast-growing field with high demand and strong job security. Key skills include network security, ethical hacking, and cloud security. Roles like analyst, penetration tester, and security engineer offer salaries from $60K to $300K+. Certifications like Security+ and CISSP boost career prospects, with a global workforce gap of over 3 million.

Cybersecurity programs cover network security, ethical hacking, forensics, and cryptography. Top schools include Carnegie Mellon, MIT, UC Berkeley, Stanford, and Georgia Tech, offering strong research and hands-on training for in-demand careers.
